Introduction to the Header

You have used anchor <a>, navigation <nav>, and unordered list <ul> tags.

Some are semantic elements, while others are non-semantic elements. Semantic elements clearly describe their meaning in a human- and machine-readable way. For instance, the <nav> element indicates that the enclosed links are for navigation, helping both search engines and developers understand the structure of the document better.

On the other hand, non-semantic elements, like <div> and <span>, do not convey any meaning about their content. They serve primarily as generic containers for grouping content and applying styles, without providing any specific context to their purpose.

Using semantic elements improves accessibility and enhances search engine optimization (SEO) by providing better context about the content's role and structure within the webpage.
